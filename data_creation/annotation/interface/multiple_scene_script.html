You will be shown the transcript of 3 scenes from a TV show. For each sentence, we have highlighted potential mentions (a word or phrase) of an entity (a person, place, or thing). This entity mention may be a pronoun (such as "she" or "their") or something else. We need your help to find an earlier mention of the same entity, whether in the same sentence or in an earlier sentence. You may select a mention by clicking and dragging your mouse pointer over the words making up the mention.

When selecting the previous mention of the entity, please follow these rules:

Select a mention of the same entity.

For example:

Joan usually comes to work early . She likes to get a head start on the day .

The highlighted entity mention in the last sentence is "She." There are two entity mentions in the preceding sentence, "Joan" and "work." We would ask you to select "Joan," because it refers to the same entity (the person named Joan) as "She" does:

Joan usually comes to work early . She likes to get a head start on the day .

Reminder: You only need to select one preceding mention instead of all of them.

Select all words in a mention.

For example:

The office manager usually comes to work early . She likes to get a head start on the day .

We would ask you to select the full mention "The office manager" by clicking and dragging over all three words, as opposed to selecting just "office manager" or "manager:"

The office manager usually comes to work early . She likes to get a head start on the day .

Reminder 1: While dragging, please only select Noun Phrases or Pronouns. Especially for th-words (this, that, there, here) and wh-words (who, what, when, which).

Reminder 2: For consistency, candidate answers in Overlapping Candidates box are preferred. If there is no suitable candidate, you could select a span by dragging.

Include possessive words as entity mentions.

For example:

The office manager does n't find her job challenging enough . She comes to work early in hopes of getting promoted .

While the entity mentions in the first sentence may appear to be "The office manager" and "her job," we also consider the possessive pronoun "her" to be a mention of the person in question. So, you may instead select "her" as a previous mention:

The office manager does n't find her job challenging enough . She comes to work early in hopes of getting promoted .

If there is no previous mention of the entity, select (p) No previous mention.

Th-words, Wh-words

Th-words (that, this, there, these, etc.) and Wh-words (what, when, which, etc.) are harder to annotate

Th-words Example 1: Charlie bought a hot dog. This hot dog was cold.

Th-words Example 2: Charlie bought a hot dog and ate this for lunch.

Th-words Example 3: Charlie thought he should buy a hot dog. This was a great idea.

In th-words example 1, the full span This hot dog is the noun phrase (This is a determiner and not a noun phrase), and so it should be labelled as (m) Not mention.

In example 2, this should be annotated with precedent mention a hot dog.

In example 3, This refers to Charlie's idea he should buy a hot dog. But we only annotate noun phrases, pronoun and possessives instead clause and sentence, so this query should be annotated as (p) No previous mention.

Wh-words Example 1: What is your plan ?

Wh-words Example 2: What if this plan wouldn't work ?

In example 1, What should be annotated with your plan. In example 2, the query should be annotated as (m) Not mention.

In the last utterance, without visual information in brackets, precedent mentions of this can not decided. So you should click (p) No previous mention and click (u) Need more visual information to be certain

In these simplified examples, we have highlighted one entity mention at a time and asked for previous mention of that entity. In general, we will select multiple entity mentions across the document (called query mentions) and ask for a preceding mention for each one. We will display the list of query mentions above the text; the active query mention will be highlighted in the text and bolded in the list above. Dragging over text will select the immediately previous mention for the active query mention only; a different query mention may be activated by clicking on it in the list of query mentions, by using the provided "next query" and "previous query" buttons, or by pressing "Tab" or "Shift" + "Tab" on your keyboard.

Additionally, we suggest entity mention selections. You click and drag to select an entity mention in the text, these mentions (called candidate mentions) that overlap your selection will be listed in a panel to the right, above the "no previous mention" button. Click on a candidate mention to change your selection.

Example:

Text
I went to a party at the bar . There was a plate of soft cheeses there . I did n't realize the bar would have such ritzy snacks .
Active query:
ritzy snacks
Answer:
soft (invalid)
Queries:
I
the bar
ritzy snacks
Overlapping Candidates

In this example, candidate mentions (or "no previous mention") have been selected for the first two query mentions. The third query mention, "ritzy snacks," is active; you may wish to select "soft cheeses" as the immediately previous mention. You could do so by clicking "(3) soft cheeses" in the right panel, pressing "3" on your keyboard, or clicking and dragging your mouse over "soft cheeses" in the text.

At any time, you may press "?" on your keyboard to see a list of keyboard shortcuts. To hide the keyboard shortcuts box, click outside the box or press "?" again.

Finally, note that you may be asked for multiple mentions of the same entity and you do not need to pick a different mention each time.

Overlapping Candidates

In this example, valid candidate mentions (or "no previous mention") have been selected for "Joan," "the office manager," and "a promotion." Additionally, "Joan" has been selected as the previous candidate mention for "she," the active query mention. A valid candidate mention has not been selected for "the day;" we'd expect you to switch the active query mention to "the day" (by clicking "the day" in the query list, clicking the "next query" button, or pressing "Tab" on your keyboard) and then select "(n)o previous mention" in the right panel (or press "n" on your keyboard). Note that "Joan" was also picked as the previous candidate mention for "the office manager," and this repetition is okay since it is correct.
